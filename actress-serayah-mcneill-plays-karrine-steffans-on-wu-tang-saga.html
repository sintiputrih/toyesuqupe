Actress Serayah Mcneill Plays Karrine Steffans On Wu Tang Saga

Karina on Wu Tang Saga is based on the actress and author Karrine Steffans. Serayah took the role of Karina in the third and final season.

The second season of the biographical drama series concluded in October 2021. The dramatized series landed in 2019 and instantly became a fan favorite for showcasing a story people have only heard.

After two successful seasons, the series was renewed for a third and final season in November 2021.

Others cast for the final season are Julian Elijah Martinez, Zolee Griggs, and Marcus Callender. The season also introduced familiar faces as guest stars, including artist King Combs portraying his father Diddy.

Moreover, Empire alum Serayah also joined the cast playing the role of Karina.

Who Is Karina On Wu-Tang: An American Saga?

Serayah plays Karina in Wu-Tang An American Saga Season 3. Serayah appeared in Episode 1, which aired on February 15, 2023.

Karrina Steffans is an American author, businesswoman, actor, and model most notable for authoring the Vixen series of books.

She has worked extensively as an actress and a video vixen and has appeared in over 20 music videos.

In 2007 and 2008, she visited several colleges to discuss her association with the hip-hop industry and its expectations of women.

People may also remember her from her appearance in F. Gary Gary’s A Man Apart, starring Vin Diesel.

In 2007, she did a spoken word introduction and epilogue in Datwon Thomas and DJ Rhude’s mixtape called Freaky Flows.

As an author, she quickly gained public attention with her first three books -2005’s Confession of a Video Vixen, 2007’s The Vixen Diaries, and 2009’s The Vixen Manual.

All three books made The New York Times Best Seller list. She later published a cautionary memoir, Confession of a Video Vixen.

Actress Serayah Makes A Guest Star In Wu-Tang: An American Saga

American actress, singer, and model Serayah McNeill appeared in Season 3 Episode 1 of the hit biographical series.

The 27-year-old actress began her acting career appearing as a DV Girl #1 in the 2014 short drama Chocolatto Is Back! The following year, she appeared in Taylor Swift’s music video for Bad Blood as Dilemma.

In July 2015, Swift even performed a rendition of her song “Style” alongside Serayah as part of her The 1989 World Tour. She further appeared in the RedOne song “Don’t You Need Somebody” in 2016.

McNeill released her debut EP entitled Addicted in 2018. She had a main role in Chris Brown’s music video for the song “Undecided.” Last year, she played in Diddy feat. Bryson Tiller’s video for Gotta Move On.

Speaking about her acting career, she is best known for playing Tiana on Empire. The production team added her to the series regular cast in August 2015.

Her other acting credits include Lucky Girl, Burning Sands, Envy: Seven Deadly Sins and Hip Hop Family Christmas.

Karrine Steffans Relationship With The Wu Tang

Karina aka Karrine has been romantically involved with famous personalities such as Lil’ Waye and Kool G Rap.

The author was previously married to Darius McCrary (2009-2011) and Columbus Short (2016).

She was only 17 when she moved in with rapper Kool G Rap. They shared a son but their relationship was tumultuous. The model later said she left Los Angeles, California, to protect her child.

Steffans has also dated Ol’ DirtyBastard and Method Man, members of the popular hip-hop group Wu-Tang Clan.

Ol’ DirtyBastard

The novelist had a brief relationship with American rapper Russell Tyrone Jones aka Ol’ DirtyBastard in 2001.

Jones was one of the founding members of the hip-hop group Wu-Tang Clan.

He began his solo career on March 28, 1995, at the age of 26.

His debut solo album, Return to the 36 Chambers: The Dirty Version, included hit singles Shimmy Shimmy Ya and Brooklyn Zoo.

The singles later helped him propel the album to platinum status.

The rapper was noted for his free-associative rhymes and outrageous profanity.

Later, Jones’s musical success was hampered by regular legal troubles. He passed away from accidental drug abuse on November 13, 2004.

Method Man

Steffans and Clifford Smith Jr. aka Method Man, a member of the East Coast hip-hop group Wu-Tang Clan, had an encounter in 2000.

Clifford is also half of the famous hip-hop duo Method Man & Redman. The Grammy Award-winning rapper is notable for his rap performance, I’ll Be There for You/You’re All I Need to Get By.

Besides being a rapper and songwriter, he is also an actor and has appeared in films.

He had recurring roles in three HBO series as Menvin “Cheese” Wagstaff in The Wire, Tug Daniels in Oz, and Rodney in The Deuce.
