Adam Silver | Career

Adam Silver was born on April 25, 1962, in Rye, New York, a suburb of New York City.

He graduated high school in 1980 and enrolled at Duke University, majoring in political science.

After graduating in 1984, he worked for a member of the US House of Representative Les AuCoin as a legislative aid.

After a year of working with Les, he went to the University of Chicago Law School, and in 1988, he graduated with a J.D degree.

He then worked for the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York Judge Kimba Wood as a clerk.

After working as a clerk for a while, he joined the law firm Cravath, Swaine & Moore as an associate.

Silver joined NBA in 1992 and served as the Deputy Commissioner and COO for eight years.

FAQs

Did Adam Silver play in the NBA?

He joined NBA in 1992 and has served in various positions, but he never played NBA himself.

What has Adam Silver done for the NBA?

Silver has been instrumental in NBA’s various signature achievements, including the three collective bargaining agreements with the NBPA (National Basketball Players Association);

The development of NBA 2k League, the creation of the NBA Africa and NBA China and the partnership with Turner Broadcasting to manage the NBA’s digital assets.

What does NBA Commissioner do?

The NBA commissioner’s job description is to oversee the activities of the organization, scheduling games, arranging the game referees, dealing with the media, and negotiating contracts.
