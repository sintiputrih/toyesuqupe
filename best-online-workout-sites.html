The Best Online Workout Sites to Stay Fit

Exercising can be challenging when you have a busy schedule. Thanks to online workout apps and programs, you can eliminate the commute and reap fitness benefits from the comfort of your home.

Based on our hours of research, we’ve selected the best online workout sites to tone up, improve your fitness levels, and get you excited about working out. Whether you’re craving yoga, looking to avoid repetitive movements, or increase your muscle strength, there’s something for everyone on our list below.

Booya Fitness

This is one of the best online workout programs for a variety of classes, including pilates, cardio kickboxing, boot camp, dance, yoga, HIIT, sports conditioning, and more. Booya Fitness also promises that you’ll only need a yoga mat for the majority of their classes.

Booya Fitness streams popular music for each workout that everyone will love. Plus, each workout song is tailored to the beat of the movement, helping to keep you moving and motivated. They currently create 60-100 new workouts a year, so you’ll never get tired of the same repetitive movements.

When you’re ready to work out, you can filter your workouts based on your mood, setting, and goals. Once you complete your workout, you’ll receive a follow-up e-mail 24 hours later with tips on what to do next, such as related workouts and rest days.

Plans are divided into three options with a one-month, three-month, or 12-month renewal. All plans give you access to the full spectrum of exercises and you’ll receive the first 30 days for free no matter which you choose.

Peloton

If you love attending group cycling classes but aren’t a fan of the immersive lights, sweaty environment, and maybe even poor-quality bikes, online cycling classes can be an excellent alternative.

Peloton cycling classes require you to have a specialized exercise bike that’s interactive to the screen, which costs a couple grand. The classes are live with other Peloton members cycling with you, plus a motivating, top instructor who you can see on the screen. You’ll receive a timetable of workouts with a set time and day for you to join.

With a range of sessions from five minutes to 90 minutes, you can squeeze in a quick exercise bike workout or vigorous routine depending on your schedule. You also have the option of a variety of themes, levels, and music genres to suit your preferences and fitness levels.

Peloton offers more than just cycling classes, including a treadmill routine for running and walking. You can also participate in pilates, body combat, yoga, and more.

For the bike workouts, the membership costs $39 per month. Alternatively, for workouts without the Peloton bike, it costs $12.99 per month.

Fitness Blender

If money is an issue, Fitness Blender offer a variety of workouts for all fitness levels for free. Alternatively, you can opt for Fitness Blender Plus for ad-free videos and help to choose your next workout if you’re feeling a little lost.

This is one of the best online workout sites for free thanks to their wide selection of routines. Their videos range from 10 minutes to two hours and are split into five levels of difficulty.

There’s a wide selection of pilates, yoga, HIIT, arm workouts, running workouts, and more. If you’re looking to target a specific part of your body, you can select the upper body or lower body option.

You can filter the videos based on what equipment you have available, and there’s plenty for exercises without a barbell, dumbbells, and mat. There’s no background music, only limited instructions, allowing you to tune up your favorite tunes to stay motivated.

ClassPass

ClassPass allows you to stream thousands of live workouts, including yoga, strength training, HIIT, and more. Compared to the other online workouts sites we’ve mentioned, you have the option of an audio or video session, depending on your instructor, the duration, and the program.

ClassPass regularly updates their workout options to keep challenging you. From all categories, you’ll receive a varied selection of duration and intensity, which all target different parts of the body.

There’s a workout for everyone — whether you’re looking to lose weight, improve flexibility, feel fitter or become stronger.

Via their free account, you can access downloadable workouts anywhere, anytime. Alternatively, purchase their monthly payment options to participate in live streaming classes with other members.

All/Out of Studio

All/Out of Studio has an abundance of categories filtered by exercises and goals. One standout category is their injury prevention section. All/Out of Studio also has the following programs: Weight loss, kettlebell, HIIT, yoga, dance cardio, agility, and more.

Taught by celebrity fitness trainers, you’ll receive advice from the best in the business who often spill their fitness secrets, so you can get the most out of your workouts. All/Out of Studio has a program for every fitness level with beginners yoga to Riptensity with Gideon Akande. Sessions range from 10 to 60 minutes, so you can always fit a workout into a busy schedule.

Their monthly subscription costs $9, or you can choose their discounted annual fee at $60. Your subscription includes tailored workouts based on your fitness levels, goals, environment and the equipment you have available.

Daily Burn

Perhaps you’re searching for an online workout site with 10 to 60-minute exercises to get sweaty to every day. Daily Burn invites members to enjoy thousands of workouts, including yoga, cardio, strength, body-training, and more.

They have pre-created workout programs for you to follow vigorously, along with advice on how often to endure the exercises. A typical program lasts four weeks, where you’ll then move onto another challenging exercise program.

You’ll receive a free 30-day trial before paying $14.95 per month for unlimited access to programs. However, you can’t begin one program until you’ve finished another, so no cheating. Your membership also includes access to audio workouts and the Daily Burn supportive community.
