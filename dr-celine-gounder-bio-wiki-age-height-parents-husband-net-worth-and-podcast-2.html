Dr. Celine Gounder Biography

Dr. Celine Gounder (born as Celine R. Gounder) is an American medical doctor and medical journalist. On November 9 she was delegated among the members of the COVID-19 Advisory Board of U.S. president-elect Joe Biden. She mostly specializes in infectious disease and global health. As of 2018, Gounder practices medicine part-time while addressing concerns with health issues as a medical journalist. In 2017, Gounder was named one of People Magazine‘s 25 Women Changing the World.

Dr. Celine Gounder Education

Moving on to her education, Gounder received her BA in Molecular Biology from Princeton University and her Master of Science in Epidemiology from the Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health. Further, Dr. Gounder also holds an MD from the University of Washington.

Previously, Dr. Gounder was an intern and resident in Internal Medicine at Harvard’s Massachusetts General Hospital. She was also a post-doctoral fellow in Infectious Diseases at Johns Hopkins University.

From 1998 and 2012, she educated herself on TB and HIV in South Africa, Lesotho, Malawi, Ethiopia, and Brazil. She was elected a fellow of the Infectious Diseases Society of America in 2016.

Dr. Celine Gounder Epidemic

Gouder’s started her career as a post-doc at Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health. While there, she researched TB and HIV in sub-Saharan Africa and served as Director for Delivery in the Consortium to Respond Effectively to the AIDS/TB Epidemic.

She later joined the New York City Department of Health and Mental Hygiene working on control of TB in the city. Dr. Gounder decided to try to address public health issues as a medical journalist and remained active in practicing medicine.

As a medical journalist, she has worked as a consultant for and on the editorial board of TEDMED and has published articles about infectious disease and other medical topics in media including The New Yorker and The Atlantic. She also appeared on TV news and talk shows. As of 2018, she was working on a documentary.

Dr. Celine Gounder Awards

Gounder received the Delta Omega Public Health Honor Society in 2000. In 2004, she was awarded the James W. Haviland Award. In 2010, she was awarded the W. Leight Thompson, MD Excellence in Research Award at Johns Hopkins.

Also in 2010, she received the Arthur M. Dannenberg, Jr. Award at Johns Hopkins. She was also an Ashoka Changemaker Finalist. In 2016, Gounder was appointed as a fellow for the Infectious Diseases Society of America.
