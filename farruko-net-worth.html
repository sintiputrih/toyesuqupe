What is Farruko’s Net Worth?

Farruko, a retired Puerto Rican singer and rapper, has a net worth of $16 million. He gained popularity with his debut album, “El Talento del Bloque,” in 2010. His albums “Visionary” and “La 167” both reached number one on the Latin charts, and his hit single “Pepas” was featured on the latter. Farruko collaborated with fellow Latin artists such as Daddy Yankee, Don Omar, and J Balvin, further contributing to his success.

Early Life of Farruko

Farruko, born Carlos Efrén Reyes Rosado, was born on May 2, 1991 in Bayamón, Puerto Rico.

Farruko’s Music Career

In 2009, Farruko started his music career by releasing his first song “Sexo Fuera del Planeta” online using Myspace to gain a following. As he continued to release music, his popularity increased, and his songs became popular on the Internet. Eventually, his music caught the attention of radio broadcasters in Puerto Rico.

Farruko’s Music Career

In 2010, Farruko released his debut album, “El Talento del Bloque,” featuring the successful singles “Nena Fichu,” “Su Hija Me Gusta,” and “Te Iré a Buscar.” He continued his success with his next album, “TMPR The Most Powerful Rookie,” in 2012, which spawned international hits like “Feel the Rhythm,” “Titerito,” and “Va a Ser Abuela.”

He released “Imperio Nazza: Farruko Edition” in 2013, which made it to number 13 on the Billboard Top Latin Albums chart and featured the singles “Besas Tan Bien” and “Voy a 100.” In 2014, Farruko achieved his first number-one hit on the Top Latin Albums chart with his album “Farruko Presenta: Los Menores,” followed by another number-one placement in 2015 with his album “Visionary.”

In 2017, Farruko released “TrapXFicante,” which peaked at number three on the Top Latin Albums chart. Two years later, “Gangalee” made it to number two on the chart. In 2021, Farruko released “La 167,” which reached the number-one spot on the Top Latin Albums chart and number 26 on the Billboard 200. The album included the hit single “La Tóxica,” which was nominated for a Latin Grammy Award for Best Reggaeton Performance, as well as the singles “Pepas” and “El Incomprendido.”

Recently, “La 167” earned a Grammy Award nomination for Best Música Urbana Album in 2022.

Farruko’s Collaborations

Farruko has achieved great success by collaborating with other Latin artists. Some of his most commercially successful collaborative songs include “Loco Enamorado,” with Abraham Mateo and Christian Daniel; “Krippy Kush,” with Bad Bunny and Rvssian; “Ponle,” with J Balvin and Rvssian; “Si se da,” with Myke Towers; “Fantasías,” with Rauw Alejandro; and “Si Me Dices Que Sí,” with Reik and Camilo.

Farruko has also been a featured artist on numerous songs, including J Balvin’s hit single “6 AM” in 2013. He has been featured on prominent songs by Chino & Nacho, Anuel AA, Yandel, Ozuna, and Sech, among many other artists. In 2021, he had a hit as a featured artist on Enrique Iglesias’s “Me Pasé.”

Reggaeton Star Farruko Blends Genres for Unique Sound

Farruko is a reggaeton artist who incorporates elements of Spanish dancehall, dembow, trival, salsa, guaracha, and EDM into his music. His unique sound showcases a blend of various genres within the Latin urban music scene.

Farruko Arrested for Hiding $50K in Undeclared Cash

In the spring of 2018, Puerto Rican singer Farruko was arrested for hiding over $50,000 in undeclared cash on his person and in his luggage when returning to Puerto Rico from the Dominican Republic. He was issued three years of probation as a consequence of the charges.

Farruko Upsets Fans with Religious Talk, Retires Singing Persona

During a concert at the FTX Arena in Miami, Florida in early 2022, Farruko upset fans by interrupting his performances to talk about his devotion to God. Many fans left the show early in dismay. Responding to complaints, Farruko announced that he would retire his singing persona and go by his birth name, Carlos. He also offered refunds to dissatisfied attendees.

Farruko’s Family

Farruko, along with his wife Kairy Lopez, has a son.
