Career as a coach:

When Frank Beamer was hired as a graduate assistant coach by the University of Maryland in 1972, his coaching career officially got underway. He started working at The Citadel as a defensive backs coach in 1973.

Beamer accepted his first position as head coach at Murray State University in 1979. From 1987 to 2015, he then went on to coach at Virginia Tech for 29 years.

Beamer is best recognized for his tenure as the football team’s head coach for the Virginia Tech Hokies. He is recognized with transforming the squad from a failing program to one of the most prosperous in the annals of collegiate football.

He guided the Hokies to five ACC championships and 23 consecutive bowl games during his time as coach. In 1999, he led the group to the national championship game, where they were defeated by Florida State.

Beamer’s approach to coaching, dubbed as “Beamer Ball,” placed a focus on special teams and a solid defense.

In addition, he was renowned for his devotion to Virginia Tech, declining multiple job offers from other institutions in order to stick with the Hokies.
