Fred Roggin is a sports anchor for KNBC-TV in Los Angeles, California. He was born on May 6, 1957, in Detroit, Michigan. He was also a sports talk radio presenter at KMPC in Los Angeles, where he co-hosted a morning sports programme with T.J. Simers of the Los Angeles Times and Simers’ daughter, Tracy Simers, until Fall 200He is now an on-air contributor at competitor KSPN-AM, providing analysis on local and national sports matters, as of June 200He hosted NBC Sports’ coverage of the 2008 Summer Olympic Games. Roggin also has a national reputation, since he works for NBC Sports on occasion. He received the call for the 1990 Escape from Alcatraz Triathlon alongside triathletes Julie  Moss and Mike Plant. He’s also been a fixture on the network’s Olympic programming. He presented daily curling coverage at the 2006, 2010, and 2014 Winter Olympics, and he was the anchor for boxing coverage from the site for the 2004, 2008, and 2012 Summer Olympics, which broadcast on CNBC and Universal HD. Before the network ended coverage following Super Bowl XXXII in January 1998, he worked as a play-by-play announcer on numerous National Football League telecasts. Roggin presents The Challenge, a new sports-themed game show that airs following NBC’s Sunday Night Football telecasts on KNBC. Roggin is also pursuing another of his ambitions: hosting a game show called The Money List, which is now being taped in the United Kingdom.
