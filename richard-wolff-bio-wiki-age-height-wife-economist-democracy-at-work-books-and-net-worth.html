Richard Wolff Biography

Richard Wolff is an American Marxian economist who was born on 1 April 1942 in Youngstown, Ohio, United States. Richard is a Professor Emeritus of Economics at the University of Massachusetts Amherst and is famously known for his work on economic methodology and class analysis. He is presently a Visiting Professor in the Graduate Program in International Affairs of the New School in New York.

Richard Economist

Richard taught at the City College of New York from 1969 to 1973. Here he joined forces with fellow economist Stephen Resnick, who arrived in 1971 after being denied tenure at Yale for signing an anti-war petition. The duo would then be part, along with Samuel Bowles, Herbert Gintis, and Rick Edwards, of the radical package that was employed in 1973 by the Economics Department at the University of Massachusetts Amherst, where Richard has been a full professor since 1981. He retired in 2008 but remains professor emeritus and that year joined The New School as a visiting professor.

Richard and Resnick first co-authored academic publication was known as The Theory of Transitional Conjunctures and the Transition from Feudalism to Capitalism, it laid out the pillars of the framework that they have worked on ever since. Their topics have covered value analysis, overdetermination, Marxian theory radical economics, social formations, the Soviet Union, and comparing and contrasting Marxian and non-Marxian economic theories, business cycles as well as international trade

Richard since 2008, gives many public lectures throughout the United States and other nations. He is a regular lecturer at the Brecht Forum. He is always a guest on television and radio news programs, and, within the United States

, has shown up on a variety of programs, as well as writing for various publications and websites. He hosts a weekly radio/TV show and podcast on economics and society, Economic Update, at WBAI in New York City.

Richard Democracy At Work

Richard is a co-founder of Democracy at Work, which is a non-profit organization that produces media and live events to uncover capitalism’s systemic problems and to show how democratizing our workplaces solves them. The organization is based on his 2012 book, Democracy at Work, A Cure for Capitalism. He also hosts the nationally syndicated program Economic Update which is produced by Democracy at Work
