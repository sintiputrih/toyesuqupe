Selena Gomez Sees Red in Armani Priv Dress at Golden Globes 2024

Selena Gomez brought the red trend into 2024 during the Golden Globe Awards on Sunday in Los Angeles. The “Only Murders in the Building” star wore a custom Armani Privé dress that featured an asymmetric hemline, bodice cutouts and sequined floral appliques.

Gomez added a pair of matching metallic pumps by Christian Louboutin and a suite of diamond jewelry from Bulgari. The actress was styled by Erin Walsh, who also dresses stars like Anne Hathaway, Jessica Alba and Elizabeth Banks.

Makeup artist Melissa Murdick gave Gomez a brown smoky eye, soft pink blush and a mauve lip. Her dark brunette tresses, done by hairdresser Marissa Marino, were swept into a stylish updo. Celebrity manicurist Tom Bachik coated Gomez’s nails in black nail polish.

Rouge hues dominated the runway during the fall 2023 season, with labels like Valentino, Carolina Herrera and Emilia Wickstead ushering in a red renaissance.

Late last year Gomez was recognized by WWD’s sister publication, Beauty Inc, which presented her with the Pete Born Impact Award for her makeup brand, Rare Beauty.

“The most important thing from the beginning of creating this brand was to have a positive impact in the beauty space — to create a brand that would just allow you to embrace who you are, and not necessarily lead people to look a certain way in order to reach ‘perfection,’” Gomez said during the Beauty Inc Awards. “Creating the mental health impact fund through Rare — that, to me, is body, mind and soul.”

Gomez is nominated for Best Performance by a Female Actor in a Television Series – Musical or Comedy for “Only Murders in the Building” at the Golden Globes. Gomez has been the owner, face and ambassador of her own beauty brand, Rare Beauty, since it launched in 2019. Previously, Gomez was a brand ambassador for Adidas, Coach, Puma and Pantene.

The 81st annual Golden Globe Awards, held at The Beverly Hilton Hotel in Los Angeles, celebrate the biggest achievements across film and television in 2023. It is produced by Dick Clark Productions, which is owned by WWD parent Penske Media Corp. Ahead of the ceremony, WWD will host its first real-time red carpet digital show, hosted by Jeannie Mai, highlighting the looks from the red carpet arrivals. On Monday, WWD will reveal the winners of its Style Awards during the red carpet recap on wwd.com/golden-globes at 10 a.m. ET.
