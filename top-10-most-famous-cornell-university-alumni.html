Top 10 Most Famous Cornell University Alumni

Cornell University, located in Ithaca, New York, is a renowned Ivy League institution that has produced some of the most successful and notable alumni in various fields. From science and technology to business and entertainment, these individuals have made significant contributions to their respective industries and have left a lasting impact on the world. Here are ten notable Cornell alumni and their life stories:

Ruth Bader Ginsburg – Supreme Court Justice

Ruth Bader Ginsburg, who graduated from Cornell University in 1954 with a degree in government, is one of the most influential figures in the legal profession. She attended Harvard Law School and later became a professor at Rutgers Law School, where she became the first tenured female professor. In 1980, President Jimmy Carter appointed her to the United States Court of Appeals for the District of Columbia Circuit, and in 1993, President Bill Clinton nominated her to the Supreme Court. She served as an associate justice for 27 years until her death in 2020.

Toni Morrison – Author

Toni Morrison, who graduated from Cornell University in 1955 with a degree in English, is a renowned author and professor. She is best known for her novel “Beloved”, which won the Pulitzer Prize for Fiction in 1988. She also received the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1993, becoming the first African American woman to receive the award. Morrison was a professor of humanities at Princeton University and later a professor of creative writing at Princeton.

Jack Dorsey – Co-founder of Twitter

Jack Dorsey, who graduated from Cornell University in 2000 with a degree in computer science, is the co-founder of Twitter, one of the most popular social media platforms in the world. He also co-founded Square, a mobile payments company, and currently serves as the CEO of both companies. Dorsey is known for his innovative approach to technology and his work in the field of mobile payments.

Paul Krugman – Economist

Paul Krugman, who graduated from Cornell University in 1974 with a degree in economics, is a renowned economist and professor. He is best known for his work on international trade and economic geography. Krugman has been a professor at various institutions, including the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and Princeton University. He has also been a regular contributor to The New York Times, where he writes a column on economic and political issues.

Maya Lin – Architect and Sculptor

Maya Lin, who graduated from Cornell University in 1981 with a degree in architecture, is a renowned architect and sculptor. She is best known for her design of the Vietnam Veterans Memorial in Washington, D.C., which she created as a student at Yale University. Lin has also designed several other notable projects, including the Women’s Table at Yale and the Civil Rights Memorial in Montgomery, Alabama.

Bill Nye – Science Educator

Bill Nye, who graduated from Cornell University in 1977 with a degree in mechanical engineering, is a science educator and television host. He is best known for his educational show “Bill Nye the Science Guy”, which aired on PBS from 1993 to 1998. Nye has also been a science advisor for several companies and organizations, including NASA and The Planetary Society. He continues to be an advocate for science education and research.

Hilary Swank – Actress

Hilary Swank, who attended Cornell University for two years, is a renowned actress and producer. She is best known for her roles in films such as “Boys Don’t Cry” and “Million Dollar Baby”, for which she won two Academy Awards for Best Actress. Swank has also produced several films and television shows, including “The Revenant” and “Madam Secretary”.

Edward Albee – Playwright

Edward Albee, who graduated from Cornell University in 1949 with a degree in English, is a renowned playwright and one of the most important figures in American theater. He is best known for his plays “Who’s Afraid of Virginia Woolf?” and “A Delicate Balance”, which both won the Pulitzer Prize for Drama. Albee has also received numerous awards and honors for his contributions to the theater, including the Tony Award for Lifetime Achievement.

E.L. Doctorow – Author

E.L. Doctorow, who graduated from Cornell University in 1953 with a degree in English, is a renowned author and professor. He is best known for his novels “Ragtime” and “Billy Bathgate”, which were both nominated for the Pulitzer Prize. Doctorow has also been a professor at several institutions, including New York University and City College of New York. He was the recipient of numerous awards and honors, including the National Book Critics Circle Award and the PEN/Faulkner Award.

Robert Moog – Inventor and Engineer

Robert Moog, who graduated from Cornell University in 1957 with a degree in electrical engineering, is a renowned inventor and engineer. He is best known for his invention of the Moog synthesizer, which revolutionized the music industry and changed the way electronic music is created and performed. Moog was also a pioneer in the field of electronic music and his contributions to the industry have been recognized with numerous awards and honors.
