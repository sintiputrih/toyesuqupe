Early Life and Career Beginnings

Whoopi Goldberg was born Caryn Elaine Johnson on November 13, 1955, in New York City. She dropped out of high school and pursued a career in theater and stand-up comedy. Goldberg’s breakthrough came with her one-woman show, “The Spook Show,” which led to her critically acclaimed role in the film “The Color Purple.”

Rise to Stardom

Goldberg’s performance in “The Color Purple” earned her an Academy Award nomination, catapulting her into stardom. She went on to star in numerous films, including “Ghost,” for which she won an Oscar for Best Supporting Actress, becoming the second black woman to win an Academy Award for acting.

Television Success

Aside from her film career, Goldberg has been a co-host on “The View” since 2007. Her role on the daytime talk show has been a significant contributor to her net worth, with reports suggesting she earns around $8 million per year from the show.

Business Ventures and Endorsements

Goldberg’s entrepreneurial spirit has led her to venture into various businesses, including a line of medical cannabis products designed to relieve menstrual pain. She has also endorsed and partnered with numerous brands throughout her career, further increasing her income.

Real Estate Investments

Real estate has been another source of wealth for Goldberg. She has bought and sold several properties over the years, often making a profit from these transactions. Her real estate portfolio has included homes in California, New York, and Vermont.

Book Sales and Writing Contributions

Theatre Productions
